About Me

Vanessa Yang is a graduate researcher specializing in human-computer interaction (HCI) with a focus on EEG-driven virtual environments and emotional regulation. Currently pursuing a Master’s in Design Technology at Cornell University, Vanessa’s work explores the intersection of neuroscience, immersive technology, and cognitive well-being, particularly for elderly populations. Her research integrates real-time EEG data with dynamic virtual reality environments to enhance emotional regulation and cognitive engagement, developing innovative interaction models that adapt to users' emotional states. Vanessa is passionate about leveraging emerging technologies such as VR and biofeedback to address societal challenges, including mental health and cognitive decline in aging communities.
